How Solar Energy Works

A simple guide to understanding your new rooftop solar system and net metering in India.

How Solar Panels Generate Electricity

It sounds like magic, but it is pure science. A rooftop solar system is made up of photovoltaic (PV) solar panels. When sunlight hits these affordable solar panels, the cells absorb the photons and generate a Direct Current (DC) electrical charge.

Because your home appliances require Alternating Current (AC), your system includes a smart solar inverter that smoothly converts this DC energy into usable AC electricity, powering everything from your lights to your air conditioners instantly.

What happens when you generate Too Much Power?

During a bright sunny day, your residential solar solution will likely generate more electricity than your home is currently consuming. Thanks to India's incredibly beneficial Net Metering system, no energy goes to waste.

Understanding net metering in India is simple: your excess solar energy is automatically exported back to the local electricity grid. The electricity provider visually measures this outward flow and credits your account, rolling back your meter.

Net Metering & Using Grid Power at Night

When the sun sets, your rooftop solar installation stops generating power. However, because you are connected via net metering, you safely draw the power you need from the city grid.

At the end of your billing cycle, the utility company looks at the energy you drew at night minus the excess solar energy you sent them during the day. If you generated enough, your electricity bill can literally drop to zero.

Enjoying Long-Term Savings

By claiming a government solar subsidy in India, your upfront costs drop drastically. Most Drasti Solar Power customers find that their affordable solar panels pay for themselves completely within 3 to 5 years.

With top-tier panels designed to last 25 to 30 years, that means you enjoy decades of totally free electricity, giving you ultimate financial stability and energy independence.
